Dosa




Description

Dosa is a crispy, savory South Indian pancake made from a fermented batter of rice and urad dal (black gram). To prepare it, soak rice and urad dal separately for several hours, then grind them into a smooth paste. Combine, add a little salt, and allow the batter to ferment overnight. Heat a flat griddle, pour a ladle of batter, and spread it in a thin circle. Drizzle a few drops of oil around the edges, cook until golden and crisp, and serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.


Ingredients

  • Rice
  • Urad dal
  • Fenugreek seeds
  • Salt
  • Water
  • For Cooking
  • Oil or ghee
  • Optional Sides
  • Coconut chutney
  • Sambar

Steps

  1. Rinse and soak rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds separately for several hours.
  2. Grind the soaked rice and urad dal into a smooth batter, adding water as needed.
  3. Combine both batters, add salt, and mix well.
  4. Cover and ferment the batter in a warm place overnight or until it rises and turns slightly airy.
  5. Heat a flat griddle or tawa on medium heat.
  6. Pour a ladle of batter in the center and spread it in a thin circle.
  7. Drizzle oil or ghee around the edges.
  8. Cook until the edges turn golden brown and crispy.
  9. Fold and serve hot with chutney and sambar.
