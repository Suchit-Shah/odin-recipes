Thepla




Description

Pav bhaji is a popular Indian street food consisting of a spicy, buttery vegetable mash served with soft bread rolls. To make it, a mix of vegetables such as potatoes, peas, and cauliflower are boiled, mashed, and cooked with onions, tomatoes, and pav bhaji masala. Generous butter and fresh coriander enhance its flavor. The dish is served with toasted pav (bread rolls) and lemon wedges for a tangy kick.


Ingredients

  • Whole wheat flour
  • Fresh fenugreek leaves
  • Yogurt or curd
  • Green chilies
  • Ginger
  • Garlic
  • Turmeric powder
  • Red chili powder
  • Coriander powder
  • Cumin powder
  • Asafoetida
  • Salt
  • Oil
  • Water

Steps

  1. Wash and finely chop fresh fenugreek leaves.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine whole wheat flour, fenugreek leaves, yogurt, chopped green chilies, ginger, and optional garlic.
  3. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, asafoetida, and salt.
  4. Pour in a little oil and mix the ingredients.
  5. Gradually add water and knead into a soft, smooth dough.
  6. Cover and let the dough rest for about 15â€“20 minutes.
  7. Divide the dough into equal portions and roll each into a ball.
  8. Roll out each ball into a thin, round flatbread using a rolling pin.
  9. Heat a tawa or flat pan on medium heat.
  10. Place a rolled thepla on the hot tawa and cook until light brown spots appear on the bottom.
  11. Flip and cook the other side, applying a little oil or ghee.
  12. Continue flipping and cooking until both sides are golden and cooked through.
  13. Serve hot with yogurt, pickle, or tea.
