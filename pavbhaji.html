Pav Bhaji




Description

Pav bhaji is a popular Indian street food consisting of a spicy, buttery vegetable mash served with soft bread rolls. To make it, a mix of vegetables such as potatoes, peas, and cauliflower are boiled, mashed, and cooked with onions, tomatoes, and pav bhaji masala. Generous butter and fresh coriander enhance its flavor. The dish is served with toasted pav (bread rolls) and lemon wedges for a tangy kick.


Ingredients

  • Potatoes
  • Cauliflower
  • Green peas
  • Onions
  • Tomatoes
  • Green capsicum
  • Ginger
  • Garlic
  • Green chilies
  • Pav bhaji masala
  • Red chili powder
  • Turmeric powder
  • Salt
  • Butter
  • Fresh coriander leaves
  • Lemon
  • For Serving
  • Pav
  • Butter

Steps

  1. Boil potatoes, cauliflower, and green peas until soft.
  2. Mash the boiled vegetables and set aside.
  3. Heat butter in a large pan or tawa and sauté chopped onions until translucent.
  4. Add chopped ginger, garlic, and green chilies; sauté until aromatic.
  5. Stir in chopped tomatoes and cook until soft and pulpy.
  6. Add chopped green capsicum and cook for a few minutes.
  7. Mix in pav bhaji masala, red chili powder, turmeric, and salt.
  8. Add the mashed vegetables and mix well.
  9. Pour in some water to adjust consistency and simmer, mashing further as needed.
  10. Garnish with butter and fresh coriander leaves.
  11. Slice pav bread rolls, toast them on a pan with butter until golden.
  12. Serve the hot bhaji with butter-toasted pav and lemon wedges.
